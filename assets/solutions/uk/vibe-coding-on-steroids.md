# Vibe coding on steroids

Instant cloud playgrounds, an AI assistant in the panel, a URL to show off — your idea, alive in ninety seconds.

You have an idea. You don't want to install Postgres, configure Docker, or pick a hosting provider before you find out whether the idea is any good. Fibe gives you a workshop where the boring parts are already done: a database, an auth layer, a deploy target, and an AI Genie sitting in the side panel that knows what you're trying to build.

## The 90-second loop

1. Describe the idea ("a list of cafes my friends like, with ratings")
2. The Genie scaffolds it — schema, routes, a minimal UI
3. You tweak the parts that matter to you in the browser
4. Press deploy, hand the URL to a friend
5. Iterate live

## Why this changes the math

Most "AI builder" tools either generate code that needs an environment, or generate an environment you can't escape. Fibe gives you **both** — a real environment **and** the AI to drive it — and lets you keep the code, take it elsewhere, or grow it into a serious product without rewriting.

## When to pick it

- You're scratching an itch and don't want infrastructure to be the hard part
- You're prototyping for a customer call this afternoon
- You teach, and you want students to ship something on day one
- You like the *vibe* of vibe coding but you want the result to actually run a year from now
