# Build in public

A live URL for whatever you're shipping — viewers, customers, future hires watch the progress.

Every Fibe project gets a sharable URL from the first commit. Not a screenshot in a tweet — the real thing, running, with the latest changes already deployed. Treat the link as your changelog, your demo, and your interview portfolio all at once.

## What it looks like

- A persistent URL like `your-thing.fibe.app` that always shows latest main
- Optional public access logs ("47 people watched the homepage land")
- Embed-friendly: tweet the URL and the unfurl shows a live preview
- Locked branches for stable demos so a broken commit doesn't blow up your launch

## Why people use it

- **Solo founders** turn shipping into content
- **Side-project builders** keep momentum visible
- **Open-source maintainers** show "this is what the next release looks like"
- **Job seekers** share something that actually works, not a screenshot

## Privacy that still matters

You decide who sees what: open to the internet, behind a passphrase, or limited to a list of verified users. Build in public doesn't mean *expose everything* — it means *make the work visible to the people who matter*.
