# Self-hosted stacks

Run the open-source equivalents of your SaaS sprawl on your own hosts. Pay for compute, not seats.

You probably pay too much for the same five categories of software every year. Fibe makes it boring to self-host the open-source versions: PostgreSQL instead of a database SaaS, Metabase instead of a BI SaaS, Plausible instead of an analytics SaaS, n8n instead of a workflow SaaS, and so on — all stitched together with the same auth, the same backups, and the same on-call story.

## What it looks like

- A catalog of vetted open-source services with one-click install
- Shared SSO so users don't reauth six times a day
- Shared backups and observability across all of them
- Upgrade paths that don't require a war room

## The math

Most teams pay $X per seat per month per SaaS, across 5–10 tools. Below a few hundred users the SaaS bill wins on convenience; above it, self-hosting wins on cost, control, and data sovereignty. Fibe makes the convenience part of self-hosting close enough that the cost curve actually matters.

## When to pick it

- Privacy or regulatory pressure makes "where is our data" a real question
- You're scaling past the price-tier where per-seat SaaS makes sense
- You want one place to apply security policy across all your tooling
