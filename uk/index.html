Evaluating an agent on real software stops being safe the moment you let it touch your own machine, your VPN, your secrets, your shared dev cluster?

Just hand it The Edge — and nothing more:

  • Hard isolation, granular control — one environment per experiment, with permissions, network egress, mounts, secrets and resource caps you set per run. Hand the agent The Edge — and nothing more.
  • Configure every agent per-experiment — skills, prompt, arguments, mounted files, tools allowed and tools blocked. Pin it in the recipe, reproduce it bit-for-bit, diff two runs in seconds.
  • Swap LLM providers like batteries — local GPU, hosted, frontier, a private endpoint, last week's model versus this week's. Same experiment, same harness, different brain — compare with a single env variable.

Capture this, don't let it slip

The boring fundamentals people miss in marketing copy — and the ones that decide whether you'd actually run Fibe long-term.

01 · Where it runs

Total control

  • Managed service — sign in, ship, never think about ops.
  • Or self-host the whole thing — laptop, home server, local GPU. Even Fibe never sees your code or your models.
  • Or mix — our web app talking to your hardware, or vice-versa. Any combination.

No Google in the loop. No Microsoft. The only third party is Fibe — and even that's opt-in.

02 · How you pay

Flat fee per server

  • One number per server — pick the box, pay the box. Whatever size, one flat fee.
  • No per-seat anything — invite 5 teammates or 500. Same bill.
  • Outgrew a tier? — just upgrade the server. New tier, same shape: one number.

Your bill scales with hardware, not headcount. Predictable on day one, predictable in year three.

03 · What it actually is

Boring core, AI on top

  • The product is environments — real, full-stack, isolated Docker hosts. Click a button or spin them programmatically.
  • Same surface everywhere — web UI, CLI, script, agent. All hitting the same APIs.
  • AI is a feature, not the point — wire it in everywhere, or never. The product still ships.

The boring part is what you actually use. The AI is what makes it five times faster.

04 · How it stays sane

Run it locally first

  • Plain docker-compose.yml — Fibe is a thin wrapper, not a custom DSL. Your repo is the source of truth.
  • Verify CI/CD on your laptop — run the whole pipeline locally. Only push to production once it's green.
  • Same compose, anywhere — your laptop, Fibe, a teammate's workstation, an air-gapped customer site. Identical behaviour.

Standard Docker. Standard Compose. No magic between them and you.
